# Global Compliance Standards & Certifications

<law_definition id="ISO-27001">
  <name>ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Information Security Management</name>
  <effective_date>2022-10-25</effective_date>

  <rule category="security_controls">
    <description>
      Requires a risk-based approach to security. Mandatory controls include Access Control (A.5.15), Crypto (A.8.24), and Logging (A.8.15). The 2022 version restructured Annex A into 93 controls across 4 themes: Organizational, People, Physical, and Technological.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="transition_status">
    <description>
      Transition from ISO 27001:2013 to ISO 27001:2022 completed — deadline was October 31, 2025. All valid certifications must now be to the 2022 version. A February 2024 climate action amendment requires organizations to consider climate change as a relevant factor in ISMS context and risk assessments.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="ISO-42001">
  <name>ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Artificial Intelligence Management System</name>
  <effective_date>2023-12-18</effective_date>

  <rule category="ai_governance">
    <description>
      Defines organizational roles for AI, risk assessments, and transparency requirements throughout the AI lifecycle. No new editions or amendments published in 2025-2026. Increasingly adopted to support compliance with the EU AI Act (enforcement wave Aug 2, 2026). Organizations are integrating ISO 42001 with ISO 27001 via combined audit programs, as both share the Plan-Do-Check-Act management system structure.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="ISO-27701">
  <name>ISO/IEC 27701:2025 — Privacy Information Management System (PIMS)</name>
  <effective_date>2025-10-01</effective_date>

  <rule category="privacy_management">
    <description>
      Updated from the 2019 version. Now a standalone certifiable standard (no longer requires ISO 27001 certification as a prerequisite, though integration is recommended). Provides a framework for managing personal information processing, mapping to GDPR, LGPD, and other privacy regulations. Covers both PII Controllers and PII Processors.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="SOC2">
  <name>SOC 2 — Trust Services Criteria</name>
  <rule category="security_controls">
    <description>
      Maps to Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy criteria.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>
