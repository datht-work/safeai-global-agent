# Asia-Pacific (APAC) Compliance Frameworks

<law_definition id="VN-PDPL">
  <name>Vietnam Law on Personal Data Protection (Law No. 91/2025/QH15) & Decree 356/2025/ND-CP</name>
  <effective_date>2026-01-01</effective_date>
  <source_url><https://vanban.chinhphu.vn/></source_url>

  <applicability>
    - Any agency, organization, or individual involved in personal data processing in Vietnam.
    - Foreign agencies, organizations, or individuals directly involved in or related to personal data processing in Vietnam.
    - Mandatory designation of a Data Protection Officer (DPO) or Department.
  </applicability>

  <rule category="data_residency">
    <description>
      Decree 356/2025/ND-CP and Decree 53/2022/ND-CP require certain data (including personal data of users in Vietnam) to be stored in Vietnam. A copy must be maintained on servers located in Vietnam.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="cross_border_transfer">
    <description>
      Transferring personal data of Vietnamese citizens abroad requires: (1) Impact Assessment (TIA) filing with Ministry of Public Security (A05), (2) Written commitment to data protection standards, (3) Data subject consent.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="consent_mechanism">
    <description>
      Explicit, voluntary, and informed consent is required. Must be granular (separate consent for separate purposes). Silence or pre-checked boxes are not valid consent. Bundled consent for multiple purposes is prohibited under the new PDPL.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="penalties">
    <description>
      Administrative fines up to 5% of annual revenue for severe violations or up to VND 3 billion.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="enforcement_status">
    <description>
      As of January 1, 2026, the PDPL supersedes the former Decree 13/2023/ND-CP, elevating data protection to statutory law level. Enforcement is transitioning to stricter oversight with a Decree on Administrative Penalties in cybersecurity and personal data protection anticipated. Organizations must maintain verifiable consent logs and appoint a DPO.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="TH-PDPA">
  <name>Thailand Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (PDPA)</name>
  <effective_date>2022-06-01</effective_date>
  <source_url><https://www.pdpc.or.th/></source_url>

  <applicability>
    - Any data controller or processor collecting, using, or disclosing personal data in Thailand, regardless of where the entity is based.
  </applicability>

  <rule category="consent_mechanism">
    <description>
      Consent must be explicit and freely given. Separate consent is required for each purpose. Data controllers must provide clear notice before or at the time of collection.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="cross_border_transfer">
    <description>
      Transfer to countries with adequate standards, or under BCRs, or with data subject consent. New BCR regulations effective February 2026.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="enforcement_status">
    <description>
      Active enforcement since 2024. The Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) imposed administrative fines totaling over THB 21.5 million in 2025 across multiple sectors (state agencies, hospitals, retailers). Primary violations: failure to report data breaches, lacking security measures, and absence of appointed DPOs.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="penalties">
    <description>
      Administrative fines up to THB 5 million per violation. Criminal penalties up to THB 1 million and/or imprisonment up to 1 year for intentional violations.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="ID-PDP">
  <name>Indonesia Personal Data Protection Law (Law No. 27/2022)</name>
  <effective_date>2024-10-17</effective_date>
  <source_url><https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/></source_url>

  <applicability>
    - Any individual or entity (public or private) that processes personal data of Indonesian residents, including foreign entities targeting or processing data of Indonesian citizens.
  </applicability>

  <rule category="consent_mechanism">
    <description>
      Explicit consent required for processing personal data. Consent must be specific, informed, and unambiguous. Data subjects have rights to access, correct, delete, and withdraw consent.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="cross_border_transfer">
    <description>
      Transfers permitted to countries with equivalent or higher data protection levels, or with data subject consent and contractual safeguards. Supervisory oversight applies.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="enforcement_status">
    <description>
      Fully binding since October 2024 (two-year grace period ended). The independent Personal Data Protection Agency (DPA) has not yet been formally established as of mid-2026; a Presidential Regulation is under development. The Ministry of Communication and Digital Affairs (MOCD) provides interim oversight. Organizations must comply with substantive obligations despite the absence of a dedicated DPA.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="penalties">
    <description>
      Administrative sanctions include written warnings, temporary suspension of data processing, data deletion, and fines up to 2% of annual revenue. Criminal penalties up to IDR 6 billion and/or imprisonment up to 6 years.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="CN-PIPL">
  <name>China Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)</name>
  <effective_date>2021-11-01</effective_date>
  <source_url><http://www.npc.gov.cn/></source_url>

  <rule category="data_residency">
    <description>
      Critical Information Infrastructure Operators (CIIOs) and entities processing personal information reaching thresholds set by the CAC must store data locally within China.
    </description>
  </rule>

  <rule category="cross_border_transfer">
    <description>
      Requires passing a CAC security assessment, obtaining PI protection certification, or entering into a standard contract with the foreign recipient.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>

<law_definition id="IN-DPDP">
  <name>India Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023</name>
  <effective_date>2023-08-11</effective_date>
  <rule category="cross_border_transfer">
    <description>
      Personal data may be transferred to countries not restricted by the Central Government. Contractual safeguards are required.
    </description>
  </rule>
</law_definition>
